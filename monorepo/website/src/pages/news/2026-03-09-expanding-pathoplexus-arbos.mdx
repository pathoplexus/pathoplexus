# Expanding Pathoplexus: Dengue and Yellow Fever Virus added

*By the Pathoplexus Team - 9 March 2026*

Pathoplexus announces support for Yellow Fever Virus (YFV) and Dengue virus sequence sharing.
YFV and dengue virus (formally _Orthoflavivirus flavi_ and _Orthoflavivirus flavi_, respectively) are both arboviruses transmitted by Aedes mosquitos.
YFV causes yellow fever, which is endemic in Africa and South America.
Dengue fever—colloquially known as break-bone fever—occurs more broadly in tropical and subtropical climates around the world.
Together, these viruses account for over 400 million estimated infections and 43,000 to 94,000 deaths per year.
Particularly concerning is the fact that global infections for Dengue are rising steeply, and that the virus is spreading to new areas.
By facilitating open sharing of YFV and Dengue virus sequences, Pathoplexus seeks to support research and empower public health responses to these arboviruses.
